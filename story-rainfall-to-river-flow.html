Time Series Data Story #1
From rainfall to river flow
When it rains, our network of precipitation sensors detects and measures the rainfall up to once every thirty seconds. This story follows that water from the sky to the river, using the Time Series Data Explorer to see how a catchment responds to a storm.
The Data Visualisation panel is where plots appear. Each chart can carry several variables, with separate left and right axes so measurements in different units can be read together.
Plot Management lists every plot and the variables within it. From here you add data, reorder series and control how each one is drawn.
We start with air temperature at the Plynlimon automatic weather station. Search for the station, pick the variable, and it's drawn on the chart.
Before trusting a series, open its record to check the station, units, sampling interval and any quality flags — so you know what the numbers represent.
Now add rainfall and river level to the same plot. With each series on an appropriate axis, the relationships between them become visible.
Zoom into a single storm to watch rainfall rise and fall, then pan along the record to compare events across the season.
The lag between peak rainfall and peak river level is the catchment's response time. Reading the two series together is how that story is told.
From a single rain gauge to the river's response, the Explorer lets you follow water through the catchment. Try the same steps with your own stations and dates.
